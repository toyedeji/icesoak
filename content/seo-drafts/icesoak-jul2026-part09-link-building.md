## Purpose

IceSoak's on-page content can be exceptional but without external backlinks it cannot displace Yelp (DA ~90) or TimeOut (DA ~70+) for competitive head terms. This document defines a link building strategy that is realistic for a directory site, sustainable without paid links or spam, and optimized for IceSoak's specific content types and market position. The goal is not mass link acquisition — it is targeted acquisition of 30–60 high-relevance links over 90 days, concentrated where they have the most ranking impact.

---

## 1. Link Building Principles for IceSoak

### What Works for a Local Directory

A local wellness directory earns links differently from a software startup or a news publisher. The winning approaches are:

1. **Data as linkbait** — IceSoak has unique verified data (studio counts, pricing, temperature ranges) that health/wellness journalists and bloggers cite when writing about local cold plunge scenes
2. **Resource page inclusion** — Recovery and wellness resource lists, local fitness blog roundups, and city wellness guides link to IceSoak as the directory source
3. **Studio partnerships** — Listed studios link back to their IceSoak profile (symbiotic; helps their own local SEO via citation)
4. **Expert commentary** — IceSoak as a primary source in local media coverage of the cold plunge trend
5. **Content-driven earned links** — City-specific informational pages (altitude recovery in Denver, heat recovery in Phoenix) earn links from local publications that don't have this content

### What Does NOT Work (Do Not Pursue)

- Link farms or private blog networks (PBN) — immediate penalty risk
- Generic "guest post on wellness sites" — produces low-relevance links, often DA < 20
- Buying links via link brokers — violates Google Webmaster Guidelines
- Press releases for link building — press release links are nofollow by default; build actual editorial coverage instead
- Blog comment links — no SEO value since 2012

## 2. Link Target Prioritization

Not all links are equal. IceSoak needs links that combine relevance, authority, and geographic specificity.

### Priority Tier 1: High-Authority + High-Relevance
Target DA 40+, clear topical relevance to wellness, fitness, or local city content

| Link Target Type | Examples | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| City wellness/fitness blogs | Denver health blogs, Seattle wellness blogs | Pitch as local data source for cold plunge articles |
| Local alt-weeklies / city mags | Westword (Denver), Seattle Weekly, Nashville Scene | Pitch cold plunge trend story with IceSoak data |
| Fitness and recovery publications | Breaking Muscle, STACK, Outside Online | Pitch science-backed articles with local tie-ins |
| Outdoor/athletic media (Seattle, Denver) | Outdoor Project, REI Co-op Journal, Trail Runner Mag | Pitch cold plunge for athletes/altitude athletes |
| Biohacker/longevity media | Examine.com, Suppversity, biohacking podcasts with web presence | Pitch as data source or guest expert |

### Priority Tier 2: Medium Authority + Strong Relevance
Target DA 25–40, niche relevance

| Link Target Type | Examples | Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| CrossFit affiliate gym blogs | Local CrossFit gym websites in IceSoak metros | Offer free listing + ask for mention/link |
| Personal trainer / RD blogs | Recovery-focused trainers with blog audiences | Data sharing or co-content |
| Local business association sites | Chamber of commerce wellness resource pages | Resource page inclusion |
| Yoga studio and boutique gym newsletters | Studios in IceSoak neighborhoods | Cross-promotion; link to IceSoak neighborhood page |

### Priority Tier 3: Studio Back-links (High Volume, Lower DA)
Target all 239 listed studios to link to their IceSoak listing page

| Action | Expected Links | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Outreach to studios: "Add IceSoak to your website" | 40–80 links | Low DA individually but builds citation network |
| Ask studios to link in their "Reviews/Press" page | 20–40 links | Some studios curate press mentions |
| Partner badge: "Listed on IceSoak" with HTML link snippet | Ongoing | Make it frictionless — provide HTML code |

## 3. Link Building Playbooks

### Playbook A: The Data Pitch

**Trigger:** A local journalist or blogger is writing about the cold plunge trend in their city.
**IceSoak's offer:** "IceSoak has verified data on [N] cold plunge studios in [City] — pricing, temperature ranges, and modalities. Happy to share data for your piece."

**Outreach template:**
```
Subject: Cold plunge data for your [City] wellness piece

Hi [Name],

I noticed you recently covered [wellness topic] in [City] — we have
verified data on [N] cold plunge studios across the [City] metro that
might be useful if you're planning a follow-up.

IceSoak (icesoak.com) is a directory of 239 recovery studios in 11 US
cities. For [City] we track modalities, pricing, and session options
with last-verified dates — something most local coverage lacks.

Happy to share a data summary or answer any questions about the local
cold plunge scene here.

[Name]
IceSoak Editorial Team
```

**Target outlets per metro:**
- Denver: Westword, 5280 Magazine, Denver Post health section, Mile High Sports
- Dallas: D Magazine, Dallas Morning News lifestyle, Fort Worth Star-Telegram
- Philadelphia: Philadelphia Inquirer health, Philly Mag, Billy Penn
- Austin: Austin Chronicle wellness, Austin American-Statesman, Do512
- Chicago: Chicago Magazine, Chicago Tribune health, Block Club Chicago
- Atlanta: Atlanta Magazine, Atlanta Journal-Constitution, Hypepotamus (tech/wellness)
- Seattle: The Stranger, Seattle Met, Seattle Times, Crosscut
- Miami: Miami New Times, Miami Herald lifestyle, Ocean Drive Magazine
- Nashville: Nashville Scene, Nashville Lifestyles, Tennessean health
- Los Angeles: LA Times wellness, LA Magazine, LAist, Well+Good (LA-specific)
- Phoenix: AZcentral, Phoenix New Times, Sonoran Living

### Playbook B: The Resource Page Inclusion

Many local wellness, fitness, and recovery-focused sites maintain "resources" or "find a studio" pages that link to directories or tools. IceSoak belongs on these pages.

**Target page types:**
- "[City] fitness and wellness resources" pages on gym or studio sites
- "Recovery resources" pages on athlete-focused sites
- "Cold plunge near me" aggregation pages in city guides
- Chamber of commerce "health and wellness businesses" directories

**Prospecting method:**
Search Google for:
- `"cold plunge" "[city]" inurl:resources`
- `"wellness directory" "[city]" "add a listing"`
- `"recovery resources" "[city]" site:.org`
- `"fitness resources" "[city]" "ice bath" OR "cold plunge"`

**Outreach template:**
```
Subject: Suggest a resource — IceSoak cold plunge directory for [City]

Hi [Name],

Your [page name] resource page at [URL] is a great reference for
[City] wellness seekers — I'd like to suggest adding IceSoak.

IceSoak lists verified cold plunge and sauna studios across [City]
with modalities, pricing, and maps: icesoak.com/cold-plunge/[city]/

Happy to provide any additional info.

[Name]
IceSoak Editorial Team
```

### Playbook C: Studio Partner Links

Every studio listed on IceSoak is a natural backlink candidate. Their incentive: a link from their own website to their IceSoak profile strengthens Google's understanding of where they're listed and can improve their own citation consistency.

**Implementation:**
1. Create a "Listed on IceSoak" partner badge (small PNG or SVG, 120×40px)
2. Provide studios with a 2-line HTML snippet:
   ```html
   <a href="https://icesoak.com/studio/[slug]/" rel="noopener">
     <img src="[badge URL]" alt="Listed on IceSoak" width="120" height="40" />
   </a>
   ```
3. Outreach sequence: email → follow-up at 10 days → final follow-up at 21 days

**Outreach template:**
```
Subject: Your IceSoak listing is live — link to it from your site?

Hi [Studio Name] team,

Your studio is listed on IceSoak at:
icesoak.com/studio/[slug]/

Many of our listed studios add a small "Listed on IceSoak" badge
to their website — it helps new customers find your IceSoak reviews
and improves your citation profile.

Here's a one-click HTML snippet to add it:
[HTML code]

Let us know if you'd like us to update any details on your listing.

IceSoak Editorial Team
```

**Expected conversion rate:** 15–25% of outreached studios add the badge = 30–60 studio back-links from 239 listings.

### Playbook D: City-Specific Informational Link Bait

IceSoak's unique, city-specific informational pages (altitude + cold plunge in Denver, extreme heat + cold therapy in Phoenix) are natural citation targets for local outdoor, athletic, and health publications that cover those topics.

**Pages that function as link bait:**
- `/denver/ice-bath-altitude-recovery-denver/` → pitch to Trail Runner Magazine, Colorado Runner, 303 Endurance
- `/phoenix/cold-plunge-phoenix-heat-recovery/` → pitch to AZcentral outdoors, Phoenix Magazine wellness, Arizona Sports news
- `/miami/contrast-therapy-sauna-cold-plunge-miami/` → pitch to Miami Herald health, South Florida wellness blogs
- `/seattle/infrared-sauna-cold-plunge-seattle/` → pitch to Seattle Met, The Stranger, Pacific Northwest sports blogs

**Pitch angle:** "We've published the only piece on cold plunge therapy specifically for [Denver/Phoenix/Miami/Seattle]'s conditions — [link to page]. Happy to have you reference it or we can provide a quote."

### Playbook E: HARO / Expert Commentary

Help a Reporter Out (HARO) and similar platforms (Qwoted, SourceBottle) send daily query emails where journalists request expert sources. IceSoak can respond as a cold plunge / wellness directory authority.

**HARO categories to monitor:**
- Health and wellness
- Sports and fitness
- Lifestyle and consumer
- Small business (for studio-specific stories)

**IceSoak's credibility hook:** "IceSoak tracks 239 verified cold plunge studios across 11 US metros and publishes the only structured database of cold plunge pricing, water temperatures, and modalities in the US."

**Response quality over volume:** Respond only to queries where IceSoak has genuinely useful data. Generic "cold plunge is great" responses don't earn links.

## 4. Link Velocity and Anchor Text Distribution

### Target Velocity (90 days)
- Month 1: 10–15 new links (studio back-links from outreach, 2–3 editorial placements)
- Month 2: 15–20 new links (resource page inclusions, more editorial placements, HARO wins)
- Month 3: 10–15 new links (informational link bait earning, ongoing studio partner links)
- **Total target: 35–50 links in 90 days**

This velocity is natural for a small-to-mid directory site. Spiking to 200 links in a month is a spam signal.

### Anchor Text Distribution Target

| Anchor Type | Target % | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Branded | 40% | "IceSoak," "icesoak.com," "IceSoak directory" |
| Partial match | 20% | "cold plunge directory," "Denver cold plunge studios" |
| Natural / generic | 20% | "here," "this directory," "cold plunge listings" |
| Exact match | 10% | "cold plunge studio Denver" |
| Navigational | 10% | "icesoak.com/cold-plunge/denver/" |

Never let exact match anchor text exceed 15% of the backlink profile — it is the clearest over-optimization signal Google penalizes.

## 5. Backlink Monitoring

### What to Track
- New links discovered (Google Search Console: Links report, Bing Webmaster Tools)
- Anchor text distribution (monitor monthly for over-concentration)
- Lost links (was the linking page updated or removed?)
- Link quality signals: if a linking domain starts appearing on spam lists, disavow

### Disavow Triggers
Build a disavow file if:
- A link comes from a domain with DA < 5 and no topical relevance
- A link appears from a clearly paid-link network (multiple .xyz or .info domains linking simultaneously)
- Google Search Console shows a manual action for unnatural links

### Monitoring Cadence
- Week 1 of each month: pull GSC links report, document new links
- Month 1: establish baseline (current backlink count, anchor distribution)
- Month 3: measure net new links against target; adjust playbook if behind

## 6. Links That Will NOT Move the Needle

For a team managing multiple tasks, it's equally important to know what NOT to spend time on:

- **Social media links (nofollow):** Twitter/X, Facebook, Reddit — zero SEO value; worth pursuing for traffic only
- **Citations in business directories without editorial content** (YP.com, Manta, Hotfrog) — minimal value at IceSoak's stage
- **Guest posts on generic "health tips" blogs** — DA 15–25, zero topical specificity, Google has algorithmically discounted these
- **Link exchanges** ("I'll link to you if you link to me") — reciprocal links are devalued; Google's algorithm detects them
- **Forum profile links** — zero value; historically spammy signal

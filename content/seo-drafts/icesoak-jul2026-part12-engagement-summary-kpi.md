## 3. Strategic Thesis (What This Engagement Bets On)

IceSoak wins by being the only US cold plunge directory with:
1. **Topical authority** — science-backed content at a depth no competitor (Yelp, TimeOut, Healthline) will produce for the cold plunge vertical
2. **Local specificity** — neighborhood-level landing pages where literally zero dedicated competitor pages exist
3. **Verified data** — temperature ranges, pricing, modalities, and last-verified dates that distinguish IceSoak from generic aggregators
4. **Structured for AI** — schema, answer-first structure, and entity-rich content that gets cited by Google AI Overviews, ChatGPT, and Perplexity as the authoritative local source

The 90-day engagement is designed to close the 4 critical gaps identified in the competitor analysis:
- Gap 1: No neighborhood pages (zero hyper-local landing pages) → 66 neighborhood pages published
- Gap 2: No best-of metro roundups → 11 roundups published
- Gap 3: No affiliate product content → 4+ review and comparison pages published
- Gap 4: Under-optimized studio pages and metro hubs → 192 pages audited and fixed

## 5. KPI Scoring Rubric

### 90-Day Success Thresholds

**Green (Engagement Successful — Continue and Expand)**
All of the following are true at Day 90:
- GSC Impressions +80% or better vs. Day 1
- GSC Clicks +60% or better vs. Day 1
- 50+ neighborhood + best-of pages indexed and generating impressions
- 25+ new referring domains
- IceSoak cited by at least 2 AI search tools

**Yellow (Partial Success — Diagnose and Adjust)**
Some but not all Green thresholds met. Acceptable reasons to remain in Yellow:
- Indexing delays (pages published but Google hasn't fully crawled — will resolve with time)
- Competitive keywords with longer ranking timelines (6–12 months is realistic for displacing Yelp on head terms)
- Content production ran behind schedule (2-week delay is within normal range)

Action in Yellow: identify which KPIs are furthest from target, run root cause analysis, adjust Month 4 plan accordingly.

**Red (Engagement Not Working — Major Pivot Required)**
If at Day 90:
- GSC Clicks are flat or declining vs. Day 1
- Fewer than 30 new pages indexed
- No improvement in average position on any metro hub page

Possible causes:
- Technical issue blocking crawl (robots.txt, noindex tags on published pages)
- Google algorithm update unfavorable to directory sites
- Content quality issues causing pages to be crawled but not ranked

## 6. Part-by-Part Summary Reference

### Part 01 — Keyword Research Framework
The universe of IceSoak keywords is estimated at 139–226 rankable terms across 11 metros in 6 archetypes. Priority order: metro head terms (Archetype A) → modality combos (B) → neighborhood terms (C) → "best of" (E) → informational (D) → transactional/membership (F). A scoring model (volume × competition gap × conversion proximity × topical cluster fit) determines publish sequence within each metro.

### Part 02 — Content Pillars
Four pillars: Science & Education (top-of-funnel authority), Find a Studio (local commercial core), Compare & Choose (mid-funnel + affiliate), Go Deeper (retention + advanced affiliate). Total content gap: 89–150 new pages. Biggest gap: 55–110 neighborhood pages in Pillar 2.

### Part 03 — Studio Page Optimization
Standard for all 239 `/studio/[slug]/` pages: 8 structural components, LocalBusiness + GeoCoordinates + BreadcrumbList + FAQPage schema, verified quick-facts block (temperature, price, modalities, last-verified date), 150–300 word description in IceSoak voice, 3–5 FAQ questions, 4 required internal links. Verification cadence: top studios every 3 months, others every 6 months.

### Part 04 — City Landing Page Strategy
Three city page types: metro hub (head term, 700–900 words, 11 exist), neighborhood page (hyper-local, 400–600 words, 0 exist → 66 to build), best-of roundup (editorial, 1,100–1,400 words, 0 exist → 11 to build). Each metro has a differentiated content hook tied to its unique environmental or cultural characteristic.

### Part 05 — Affiliate Integration
Five affiliate partners: Amazon Associates, Plunge, Sweaty Yeti (sId=16 required on all links), AWIN (publisher 2982909), Ice Barrel. Primary affiliate pages: best cold plunge tub guide, product comparison, individual reviews. Affiliate links categorically excluded from studio pages and best-of roundups. FTC disclosure required above first link on every affiliate page.

### Part 06 — On-Page SEO Standards
Authoritative standard for title tags (50–60 chars, formula by page type), meta descriptions (140–160 chars, CTA required), heading structure (one H1, logical H2 hierarchy), URL patterns (lowercase, hyphens, hierarchy-matched), schema by page type, Core Web Vitals targets (LCP < 2.5s, INP < 200ms, CLS < 0.1), image optimization (WebP, explicit dimensions, descriptive alt text), anchor text rules, canonical handling, OG/social tags, and a 20-point pre-publish checklist.

### Part 07 — Internal Linking Architecture
Four-tier hub-and-spoke model: root → metro hubs → neighborhood pages → studio pages. Required link sets defined for each page type. Three critical missing links identified: studio pages not linking up to metro hub, guide pages not linking to city pages, no neighborhood pages exist. Link density guidelines by page word count. PageRank flow model showing guides as the highest-leverage fix.

### Part 08 — Competitor Gap Analysis
Four competitor categories: general aggregators (Yelp), individual studio sites, national health publishers, local city guides. Content gap matrix identifies: neighborhood pages (zero competition anywhere), best-of roundups (IceSoak can beat city guides with verified data + schema + cluster), city-specific informational pages (zero national competitor content exists). SweatHouz is the only active individual brand competitor.

### Part 09 — Link Building
Five playbooks: data pitch to local media, resource page inclusion, studio partner links ("Listed on IceSoak" badge), city-specific link bait from informational pages, HARO expert commentary. Target: 35–50 new referring domains in 90 days. Anchor text distribution: 40% branded, 20% partial match, 20% natural/generic, 10% exact match, 10% navigational.

### Part 10 — GEO/AI Search Optimization
AI search features (Google AI Overviews, Perplexity, ChatGPT) are a material traffic source for wellness queries. IceSoak's structured, verified data is inherently well-suited for AI citation — IF implemented correctly. Five GEO principles: answer-first structure (quick-answer box on every page), entity-rich content (named businesses, prices, temperatures), structured data (speakable schema, DefinedTerm for technical concepts), freshness signals (dateModified updated), citation-ready claim format (specific, falsifiable facts). Robots.txt must not block GPTBot, PerplexityBot, or ClaudeBot.

### Part 11 — 90-Day Execution Roadmap
Week-by-week action plan across four tracks (Content, Technical, Links, Analytics). Month 1: fix and audit. Month 2: expand with best-of roundups and affiliate content. Month 3: add depth with protocol guides and product reviews. Total new pages: 93. Total pages touched: ~192. Three decision gates at Days 30, 60, and 90.
